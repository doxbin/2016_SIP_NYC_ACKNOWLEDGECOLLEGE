Major Keys for Staying Safe on Campus

Being in college is a very exciting and fun experience, but that experience can become less enjoyable when you experience some forms of crime. For example getting robbed, attacked, or worse. Even though safety is colleges priority, there are some things that you can do to improve your own safety. Before going to college or even if you are in college,read these great tips to improve your own safety:



Familiarize yourself with your school’s Campus Safety office.
- Getting and actually studying the school map is truly a good thing
Tell others you trust where you are going and when you’ll be back.
- Do not tell a random person to tell you friend that you are going somewhere. Text or call them so that they know
Put emergency numbers in your cell phone
- If your wallet is stolen, will you know what phone number to call to cancel your credit cards?
Keep an eye on your property at all time.
- If you are going to the bathroom or even getting a quick snack, take your stuff with you.
Get a lock for your laptop computer.
- Your laptop is your life and if someone takes it you'll lose alot of data. You can protect your laptop with a lock or a tracking device
Don't let anyone into your hall or apartment building that you don't know
- stranger danger!! Not letting someone in doesn't make you look like a jerk.
Keep an eye on your keys.
- Don't lose the keys to the kingdom. Report it, pay the fine a get a new set
Keep doors and windows locked at all times
Yes, even if you are going out for a quick minute. You don't leave you house door wide open.
Take extra precaution at night
- when coming home from a fun party make sure you have a buddy to go home with. You don't know what dark creatures dwell in the dark.
Do Not drink and drive
After having to much to drink call, a cab or stay where you are until you are sober enough
Learn how to defend yourself.
Many colleges offer self-defense classes and some have special self-defense classes for women. Take advantage of this and attend the class.
Use your common sense
If something does not feel right most likely it isn't
