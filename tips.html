Major Keys for Staying Safe on Campus

Being in college is a very exciting and fun experience, but that experience can become less enjoyable when you experience some forms of crime. For example getting robbed, attacked, or worse. Even though safety is colleges priority, there are some things that you can do to improve your own safety. Before going to college or even if you are in college,read these great 9 tips to improve your own safety:



1) Familiarize yourself with your school’s Campus Safety office

Getting and actually studying the school map is truly a good thing.

2) Keep doors and windows locked at all times

Yes, even if you are going out for a quick minute. Don't leave you door wide open for criminals.

3) Put emergency numbers in your cell phone

If your wallet is stolen, will you know what phone number to call to cancel your credit cards?

4) Keep an eye on your property at all time

If you are going to the bathroom or even getting a quick snack, take your stuff with you.

5) Keep an eye on your keys

Don't lose the keys to the kingdom. Report it, pay the fine, and get a new set.

6) Don't let anyone into your dorm that you don't know

Stranger danger!! Not letting someone in doesn't make you look like a jerk.



7) Tell others you trust where you are going and when you’ll be back

Do not tell a random person to tell you friend that you are going somewhere. Text or call them so that they know.

8) Take extra precaution at night

When coming home from a fun party make sure you have a buddy to go home with. You don't know what dark creatures dwell in the dark.

9) The most important one: Use your common sense!

If something does not feel right most likely it isn't. Trust your instincts!
